About Me

Welcome! I am a graduate student pursuing a Master of Science in Mathematics at Tarleton State University, with an expected graduation in May 2026. I specialize in longitudinal and survival data analysis, Bayesian inference, mathematical epidemiology, and network analysis.

I earned my Bachelor of Technology in Health Statistics with First Class Honors from Kumasi Technical University, Ghana, where I graduated with a GPA of 3.89/4.00. My undergraduate studies included an exchange program at Sivas Cumhuriyet University, Turkey, through the prestigious Erasmus Mundus Exchange Program.

My research focuses on the intersection of biostatistics and public health, with particular expertise in infectious disease modeling and interdisciplinary public health research. I am proficient in Python, R, MATLAB, and LaTeX, and I am passionate about applying advanced mathematical and statistical methods to solve real-world health challenges.
