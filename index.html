About

I develop adaptive infectious disease surveillance systems that integrate Electronic Health Records, network structures, and Bayesian inference to improve early outbreak detection and strengthen pandemic preparedness. My work sits at the intersection of statistical modeling, machine learning, and public health.

I hold an MS in Mathematics from Tarleton State University (4.00 GPA) and a BTech in Health Statistics from Kumasi Technical University (First Class Honors). Starting August 2026, I will begin my PhD in Computer and Statistical Sciences at the University of Rhode Island.

Research

Synergistic Disease Surveillance

with Dr. Jose Herrera Diestra · 2025–Present

Integrating network-based and network-free surveillance strategies with Bayesian mixing for early outbreak detection using EHR data.

Network-Informed Bayesian Epidemic Monitoring

with Dr. Scott Cook · 2025

Built a BayesFlow framework achieving posterior inference in under 1 second across 50,000 simulations, with 90.6% network coverage while monitoring only 1% of the population.

TB–COVID-19 Co-Infection Dynamics

with Dr. Christopher Mitchell · 2025–Present

Formulated 11 nonlinear differential equations modeling coinfection dynamics, invasion reproduction numbers, and control strategies. Under review at Scientific Reports.
