About

I develop adaptive infectious disease surveillance systems that integrate Electronic Health Records, network structures, and Bayesian inference to improve early outbreak detection and strengthen pandemic preparedness. My work sits at the intersection of statistical modeling, machine learning, and public health.

I hold an MS in Mathematics from Tarleton State University and a BTech in Health Statistics from Kumasi Technical University. Starting August 2026, I will begin my PhD in Computer and Statistical Sciences at the University of Rhode Island.

Research Interests: Infectious disease surveillance, Bayesian inference, mathematical biology, network analysis, machine learning, spatio-temporal modeling.

Education: PhD Computer and Statistical Sciences (incoming) — University of Rhode Island · MS Mathematics — Tarleton State University · BTech Health Statistics — Kumasi Technical University.
