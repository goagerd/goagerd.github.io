I am a statistician and mathematician with research interests in Bayesian inference, longitudinal and survival data analysis, mathematical epidemiology, and network-based disease surveillance. I am currently completing an M.S. in Mathematics at Tarleton State University (GPA: 4.00), where my research focuses on network-based and network-free surveillance strategies for infectious disease modeling. My work combines stochastic epidemic modeling, Bayesian inference, and large-scale network simulations to study early outbreak detection and optimal surveillance design.
