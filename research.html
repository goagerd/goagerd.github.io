Research

Developing adaptive infectious disease surveillance systems at the intersection of statistics, machine learning, and public health.

Synergistic Disease Surveillance Using EHR Data & Network Structure

with Dr. Jose Herrera Diestra · Tarleton State University · 2025–Present

Developing a synergistic approach for early disease surveillance using network-based and network-free surveillance strategies. Incorporating a Bayesian approach to dynamically adjust the mixing parameter as the population structure changes.

Network-Informed Bayesian Surveillance for Epidemic Monitoring

with Dr. Scott Cook · Tarleton State University · 2025

Developed a Bayesian Deep Learning framework (BayesFlow) integrating network science and simulation-based inference for epidemic surveillance. Achieved amortized posterior inference in under 1 second across 50,000 pre-trained simulations — vastly outperforming traditional MCMC. Engineered network-informed priors that attained 90.6% network coverage while monitoring only 1% of the population, a 2x efficiency gain. Simulation-Based Calibration confirmed 95% credible intervals achieved 93–94% coverage across 500 independent runs.

Mathematical Analysis of TB–COVID-19 Co-Infection Dynamics

with Dr. Christopher Mitchell · Tarleton State University · 2025–Present

Formulated a system of 11 nonlinear differential equations capturing TB–COVID-19 coinfection dynamics, invasion reproduction numbers, competitive dynamics, and control strategies. Under review at Scientific Reports.

Predicting Diabetic Retinopathy Using Machine Learning

with Dr. Scott Cook · Tarleton State University · 2025

Utilized AutoFLAML to identify optimal algorithms for diabetic retinopathy prediction from clinical data.

Predictive Modeling of Breast Volume Using Anthropometric Measurements

with Dr. Jesse Crawford · Tarleton State University · 2024

Developed regression-based predictive models (stepwise and polynomial) for breast volume estimation using a comprehensive dataset collected in Ghana.
