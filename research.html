Research Interests

My research spans multiple areas at the intersection of mathematics, statistics, and public health:

Longitudinal & Survival Data Analysis

Developing advanced statistical models for analyzing time-to-event data and repeated measurements in health research.

Bayesian Inference & Mathematical Epidemiology

Applying Bayesian methods and mathematical modeling to understand and predict disease transmission dynamics.

Network Analysis & Disease Surveillance

Investigating network-based and network-free surveillance strategies for epidemic monitoring and control.

Machine Learning in Healthcare

Utilizing machine learning approaches for disease detection, prediction, and improving health outcomes.

Maternal & Child Health

Statistical modeling of maternal mortality, malnutrition, and other critical health issues affecting women and children.

Current Research Projects

Network-based and Network-free Surveillance Strategies: A Synergetic Approach

August 2025 - Present

Advisor: Dr. Jose Herrera Diestra, Tarleton State University

Developing Bayesian network-based and network-free surveillance strategies by simulating SEIR epidemic models across 10,000-node networks. Conducting stochastic analyses and applying Bayesian inference to optimize surveillance node selection and performance.

Mathematical Analysis of TB–COVID-19 Co-Infection: Invasion Reproduction Numbers, Competitive Dynamics, and Control Strategies

February 2025 - Present

Advisor: Dr. Christopher Mitchell, Tarleton State University

Formulated a system of 11 nonlinear differential equations capturing TB–COVID-19 coinfection dynamics, invasion reproduction numbers, competitive dynamics, and control strategies.
